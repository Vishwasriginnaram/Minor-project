RAAG PATDEEP
Raga Patdeep is a Hindustani classical raga from the Kafi Thaat. It is a flittering lighter melody that reflects eagerness, implicit intrigue, and separation pangs. It is an Audava-Sampoorna raga, implying that it has 5 notes in Arohana and 7 in Avarohana.

RAAG JAUNPURI
Raag Jaunpuri is a Shadhav-Sampurna raga in Hindustani classical music, belonging to the Asavari thaat. It is a popular raga known for its sweet and devotional quality, often used to express Bhakti (devotion) and Shringar (beauty) rasas.

RAAG TILANG
Raag Tilang is a popular Hindustani raga that belongs to the Khamaj Thaat. It is a folk-derived raga that features distinctive double-Ni interplay, seemingly of ancient Southern origin.It is a very sweet and melodious raga.

RAAG DESHKAR
Raag Deshkar is a Hindustani classical music raga that belongs to the bilaval thaat.It is similar to Raag Bhoopali but it is different in the way it is sung. The important note here is Shuddha Dhaivat, which is very strong in Deshkar.

RAAG MALKAUNS
Raag Malkauns is one of the oldest ragas in Indian classical music, known for its majestic and serene quality. It is a pentatonic raga, meaning it has five notes, and is often rendered in the lower octave.

RAAG HAMEER
Raag Hameer is a nocturnal Hindustani classical raga that evokes feelings of courage, bravery, and enthusiasm. It is a powerful and dynamic raga that is often performed at night, typically between 9:00 pm and 12:00 am.
